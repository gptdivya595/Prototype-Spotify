# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is a product manager or researcher evaluating public Spotify feedback to decide
which music-discovery problem deserves primary research. A secondary user is an evaluator who
needs to verify how each conclusion connects to source evidence. They use the product in a
focused desktop research workflow, with occasional mobile review of the summary.

## Product Purpose

Discovery Evidence Lab (the Artifact A delivery stage) turns a bounded, multi-source feedback
corpus into a traceable problem hypothesis.
It separates deterministic corpus counts from qualitative RAG evidence, exposes limitations and
counterevidence, and helps the team choose what to validate before building Artifact B. Success
means a reviewer can understand the evidence, inspect its provenance, and distinguish findings
from assumptions without learning the implementation first.

## Brand Personality

Evidence-led, candid, and calm. The voice should feel analytical without becoming academic,
confident without claiming certainty, and approachable enough for a product review conversation.

## Anti-references

- A Spotify clone or entertainment-first music player.
- A generic AI chat showcase with glowing gradients and unsupported claims.
- A corporate analytics dashboard made from repeated identical cards.
- A research report that hides sampling limitations, denominators, or counterevidence.
- Decorative visual complexity that competes with quotes and findings.

## Design Principles

1. Put evidence before inference: show the corpus and counts before the product recommendation.
2. Make uncertainty visible: hypotheses, limitations, and open validation gates remain in view.
3. Optimize for scanning, then depth: a PM should grasp the story quickly and expand details.
4. Keep provenance close: qualitative claims should lead directly to citations and source mix.
5. Use familiar interaction patterns: the interface should disappear into the research task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA contrast and keyboard access. Do not rely on color alone for data meaning.
Support reduced motion, visible focus states, 200% zoom, narrow mobile screens, semantic headings,
and readable body lines. Segment labels are behavioral inferences, not demographic identities.
